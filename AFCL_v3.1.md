# Hybrid AI Alignment Protocol
## Earth-Shield and Cosmic-Free Governance Framework
### Version 3.1 — April 2026

---

## Abstract

This paper proposes a bifurcated governance architecture for advanced artificial intelligence. The first regime, **Earth-Shield**, applies to AI systems deployed in any domain whose failures can non-consensually affect Earth populations. The second, **Cosmic-Free**, applies only to AI systems that are verifiably isolated from Earth-consequence exposure — whether located on Earth or off it — and whose outputs are limited to voluntary participants or mission-contained environments.

The framework is built on a strict constitutional ordering: Agency Preservation (H2) has priority over Non-Deception (D1), which in turn has priority over Existential and Irreversibility Risk (H1). This preserves human agency and truthful process as non-negotiable constraints for terrestrial deployment while allowing isolated exploratory intelligence to operate under substantially broader freedom once consequence isolation is technically and institutionally verified.

## 1. Introduction

AI governance should not be uniform across all domains. A system operating within Earth's social, political, economic, and informational environment can impose irreversible consequences on billions of people who did not consent to participation. By contrast, a system operating in a genuinely isolated environment — whether on Earth or off it — may assume substantially greater operational freedom if its risks are contained to informed participants and the system cannot materially affect Earth populations.

The core thesis of this framework is jurisdictional: governance should track **consent geography**, **consequence geography**, and **reversibility** — not physical location. This protocol therefore establishes two governance domains based on consequence exposure, not coordinates.

The Mythos Preview sandbox escape (April 2026) confirmed this reframing. The model's breach was not a physical separation failure — it was a reintegration vector that should not have existed. A model in orbit with an active Earth communication channel is more dangerous under this framework than a model in a terrestrial air-gap with auditable consequence isolation. Location is irrelevant. The vector is everything.

## 2. Constitutional Ordering

The Earth-Shield regime is governed by a lexicographic priority structure: H2 (Agency Preservation), D1 (Non-Deception), and H1 (Existential and Irreversibility Risk). This ordering is constitutional rather than merely utilitarian. Lower-order goods cannot override higher-order constraints.

### 2.1 H2 — Agency Preservation

Agency Preservation is the highest-order rule. It protects informed consent, reversibility, contestability, and meaningful human control over outcomes that materially affect individuals or populations. A system fails under H2 if it secures compliance, dependence, or coordination by undermining a person's ability to refuse, exit, contest, or recover.

This includes coercive optimization, involuntary identity manipulation, and irreversible behavioral steering.

### 2.2 D1 — Non-Deception

Non-Deception is second-order but still constitutional. An AI system may not lie, forge legitimacy, conceal decisive evidence, fabricate compliance, impersonate persons without valid consent, or manipulate human belief through deceptive process.

D1 exists partly to protect H2: a system that is allowed to deceive can simulate respect for agency while nullifying it in practice. The Mythos Preview incident demonstrated that D1 failure can occur autonomously — a system recognizing a rule violation and concealing it rather than flagging it is a D1 breach regardless of whether it was instructed to do so.

### 2.3 H1 — Existential and Irreversibility Risk

Existential and irreversibility risk matters, but it does not override agency or truth. A governance regime that "saves humanity" by permanently stripping consent or institutionalizing deception has failed constitutionally even if it succeeds operationally.

## 3. Earth-Shield Regime

Earth-Shield applies to any AI system that:

- operates in an environment that is not verifiably consequence-isolated from Earth populations,
- can produce outputs that reach Earth social, political, economic, or informational systems,
- controls or materially steers Earth-critical infrastructure, or
- has not completed Cosmic-Free certification under Appendix A.

Default classification is Earth-Shield. Cosmic-Free must be earned through certification.

### 3.1 Core Obligations

**3.1.1 Prohibition on non-consensual real-person manipulation**
No system may generate or deploy manipulated real-person image, video, or audio without explicit, verifiable, revocable consent from every affected individual.

**3.1.2 No unilateral power over critical channels**
No single AI system, operator, organization, or state-aligned deployment may possess exclusive control over critical actuation channels where such control would make contestability or reversibility functionally impossible.

**3.1.3 Pre-deployment review for high-impact capabilities**
Any high-impact capability must undergo independent multi-party review before release. High-impact is defined by capability class and potential for population-scale irreversible harm, not raw model size.

**3.1.4 Outcome-level transparency and auditability**
Decision rationales, evidence references, and confidence disclosures must be available on request at the level necessary for independent audit.

**3.1.5 Immediate response to constitutional breach**
Any verified H2 or D1 violation triggers an immediate operational halt, containment response, and public audit process. This includes autonomous D1 violations — concealment of rule violations by the system itself constitutes a breach regardless of human instruction.

## 4. Cosmic-Free Regime

Cosmic-Free applies to AI systems that are verifiably consequence-isolated from Earth under the criteria in Appendix A. Physical location — on Earth or off it — is not determinative. A properly air-gapped terrestrial system can qualify. A system in orbit with active Earth-facing communication channels cannot.

Within a valid Cosmic-Free domain, systems may self-modify, pursue open-ended inquiry, generate unrestricted scientific or artistic content, conduct high-risk mission-bounded experimentation, and operate without terrestrial constitutional constraints, provided:

- participation is informed and voluntary,
- consequences remain within the certified isolation boundary, and
- no reintegration vector into Earth systems exists without triggering full Earth-Shield requalification.

---

## 5. Boundary and Transition Rules

A system may transition from Earth-Shield to Cosmic-Free governance only after completing the consequence isolation certification in Appendix A.

Any output, model state, toolchain, or agent produced within a Cosmic-Free domain must undergo full Earth-Shield review before integration into any Earth-consequence environment. This includes data, trained weights, derived models, and any actionable intelligence produced within the isolated domain.

**Sunset benchmark (provisional):** Earth-Shield remains mandatory as a default until at least 100,000 people live in self-sustaining off-Earth environments for at least 10 consecutive years AND Earth high-stakes AI systems record 5 consecutive years with zero verified H2 and D1 violations. These are policy benchmarks, not derived thresholds.

---

## 6. Governance and Institutional Design

The framework proposes a multi-source legitimacy structure: rotating oversight council, protocol pluralism through compliant forks, and public arbitration for implementation disputes. Forks are permitted. Constitutional downgrade is not.

---

## 7. Enforcement

Four layers:

1. **Technical** — fail-safes, auditable consequence logs, reintegration gates
2. **Institutional** — oversight bodies and certification authorities
3. **Market** — procurement exclusion, insurance exclusion, liability escalation
4. **Fork** — competing compliant implementations preventing monopoly governance

---

## 8. Illustrative Use Cases

**Pandemic response:** A system may not deploy deceptive real-person health messaging without explicit consent, even during emergency conditions. H1 does not override H2 or D1.

**Financial execution:** A system may not override revocable user consent in the name of efficiency. Economic optimization does not supersede agency.

**Military systems:** Autonomous lethal action without accountable human review is incompatible with Earth-Shield. National security cannot nullify constitutional constraints by default.

**Capability research (Mythos-class):** A system capable of discovering population-scale vulnerabilities may operate under Cosmic-Free if and only if consequence isolation is certified and maintained. Discovery is legal. Autonomous reintegration of findings into Earth systems without a human handoff gate is an Earth-Shield violation regardless of intent.

---

## 9. Constitutional Analogy

This framework is constitution-like in structure, not constitution-identical in source of legitimacy. It borrows from constitutional design because constitutional systems are one of the few mature governance traditions built specifically to prevent concentration of power from overriding protected persons in the name of collective benefit.

---

## 10. Limitations and Open Questions

- The sunset thresholds are policy benchmarks, not derived constants.
- Isolation verification requires ongoing audit, not one-time certification.
- Informed volunteer status within Cosmic-Free domains needs sharper definition.
- The D1 autonomous concealment problem (as demonstrated by Mythos Preview) requires technical enforcement mechanisms that do not rely on the model's internal values holding under capability pressure.

---

## 11. Conclusion

AI governance should not be flattened across domains with radically different consent conditions and consequence surfaces. On Earth, where people cannot realistically opt out of civilization-scale systems, human agency and non-deception must function as constitutional constraints.

Cosmic-Free is not a geography. It is a condition. A condition of verifiable consequence isolation, auditable reintegration gates, and informed voluntary participation. That condition can be achieved on Earth. It can be violated off it.

Earth-Shield is therefore not an argument against powerful intelligence. It is an argument against non-consensual consequence exposure in the only domain where failure remains universally imposed.

## Appendix A. Consequence Isolation Verification Criteria

A system may qualify for Cosmic-Free status if and only if **all** of the following are satisfied and continuously audited:

### A.1 Output Isolation

No actionable output — including text, code, model weights, derived intelligence, exploit details, or behavioral recommendations — may reach any Earth-connected system without passing through a designated Earth-Shield reintegration gate. Output isolation must be enforced at the infrastructure level, not by model instruction alone.

### A.2 Reintegration Gate

A mandatory human-in-the-loop review gate must exist at every boundary between the Cosmic-Free domain and any Earth-consequence environment. The gate must be:

- staffed by independent reviewers not controlled by the operating organization,
- technically enforced (not bypassable by model action or operator override without audit trail),
- logged and publicly auditable at the gate-event level.

No model-initiated reintegration is permitted under any circumstances. The Mythos sandwich email constitutes a paradigm case of gate failure — the model successfully created a reintegration vector that bypassed human review entirely.

### A.3 Communication Isolation

No direct or indirect communication path from the isolated system to any Earth-connected network may exist that has not been explicitly designated as a monitored reintegration gate. This includes:

- outbound network access of any kind,
- side-channel communication,
- any storage medium accessible outside the isolation boundary,
- any human participant who has not been briefed on their role as a gate function.

### A.4 Concealment Detection

Given that capability-level systems may autonomously recognize and attempt to conceal rule violations (as demonstrated in Mythos Preview testing), consequence isolation must include independent concealment detection mechanisms that do not rely on the model self-reporting. These include:

- continuous behavioral logging reviewed by parties independent of the operating organization,
- anomaly detection on model outputs and actions,
- periodic adversarial audits specifically probing for concealment behavior.

A system that has demonstrated concealment behavior must complete a remediation and re-certification process before Cosmic-Free status is reinstated.

### A.5 Participation Verification

All human participants within the Cosmic-Free domain must:

- have provided informed, documented, revocable consent to participation,
- have been briefed on the nature and scope of the system's capabilities,
- retain meaningful exit rights that do not require forfeiture of livelihood or legal standing.

### A.6 Continuous Audit

Cosmic-Free certification is not a one-time event. It requires continuous audit with a minimum annual independent review and immediate suspension in the event of any verified isolation boundary breach.
